Shield + Monogram

The TS Crest

A shield says trust, protection, authority. An intertwined TS monogram says identity. Together they say: "We've got this." Six approaches — from classic heraldic to cutting-edge geometric.

SH-1
The Crest
Shared stem — T and S are one structure
Classic heraldic shield outline. The T's vertical stem runs through the center and the S attaches to it — the S's horizontal bars extend left and right from the shared spine. The T is white, the S is cyan. They literally share the same backbone. Reads as "TS" immediately.

SH-2
The Aegis
Block letters — T behind, geometric S in front
Hexagonal shield shape for a tech/cyber feel. A bold white T sits behind a geometric, angular S made entirely of straight lines and right angles — no curves at all. The S overlaps the T, creating depth. The angular S feels like a circuit board trace. Both letters are heavy and confident.
Angular Tech Bold

SH-3
The Guardian
Flowing ligature — T and S share one stroke
Modern rounded shield. The T crossbar flows seamlessly into the top half of the S — they share the same continuous stroke. The T stem drops down through the center. The S bottom half completes in cyan. It reads as one connected TS letterform, not two separate letters. Elegant, premium, fluid.
Fluid Premium Elegant

SH-4
The Sentinel
Negative space — TS carved from a solid shield
A solid cyan shield with the TS monogram carved out as negative space — the dark background shows through the letter shapes. The T and geometric S overlap and merge into a single cut-through pattern. Bold, dense, premium. The shield IS the logo — the letters are what's missing from it. Inverted thinking.
Negative Space Dense Unique

SH-5
The Emblem
Interwoven — T and S weave over/under each other
Thin shield outline, all focus on the monogram. The T and S physically interweave — the T stem passes over and under the S bars, like a Celtic knot or woven fabric. Creates a sense of two things working together as one. The most literally "intertwined" concept. Sophisticated, detailed, craft-quality.
Interwoven Crafted Sophisticated

SH-6
The Bastion
Split shield — T left, S right, big and bold
The simplest, most direct approach. A heavy filled shield with massive T and S letters side by side — the T in white, the S in cyan. They overlap naturally where they meet at the center. A faint vertical divider suggests the shield is split like a traditional coat of arms. Massive presence, zero ambiguity. Reads "TS" from across a room.
